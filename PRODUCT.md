# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three audiences in practice: (1) people in the theater/opera/film industry sizing up Robert Falls for a project — producers, journalists, casting directors, students — who need to register his range and credibility fast; (2) press, historians, and Chicago-theater enthusiasts researching a specific production or era; (3) family and personal contacts checking in on what he's currently working on. None of them are passive browsers — they arrive with a specific question ("is he still working," "what did he direct," "is this the same Robert Falls who did X") and need it answered within the first screen.

## Product Purpose

This is Robert Falls's personal site: a working theater director's home base, not a memorial or a retirement scrapbook. He stepped down as Goodman Theatre Artistic Director in 2022 after 35 years but is actively freelancing (Amadeus at Steppenwolf 2025, Holiday at the Goodman 2026, a third Lyric Opera revival of Don Giovanni opening October 2026) and explicitly does not want to be framed as retired. Success means a visitor leaves believing two things at once: this person has an enormous, serious body of work, and this person is still actively directing right now. The site has grown a ~90-page production archive (full cast/creative/reviews/galleries), a reverse-chronological career timeline (1954-2026), credits, press, and awards pages — the homepage's job is to be the front door that proves the depth exists and pulls visitors into it, not to duplicate it.

## Brand Personality

Authoritative, literary, weighty. This reads like the program insert for a major regional theater, not a marketing site or an actor's resume page. Confidence comes from restraint and specificity (real numbers, real quotes, real dates) rather than enthusiasm or salesmanship. Serif italic pull-quotes, oxblood/gold/cream/ink palette, grain texture, and dramatic display type are already established and should be extended, not reinvented.

## Anti-references

Must not look like a generic actor/director portfolio template (headshot + bio + résumé list, the default Squarespace/WordPress theatrical-portfolio look). Must not look like a corporate SaaS landing page (hero-metric tiles, gradient text, identical icon-card grids). Must not read as a retirement retrospective or memorial site — even sections about the 35-year Goodman tenure should feel like evidence of ongoing standing, not eulogy.

## Design Principles

1. **Prove range, don't just claim it.** Specific titles, specific collaborators, specific numbers beat adjectives like "legendary" or "acclaimed."
2. **Now, then legacy.** Current/upcoming work earns the top of the page; the 35-year Goodman tenure is the foundation that makes the current work credible, not the headline.
3. **The archive is the asset.** With ~90 fully-built production pages behind it, the homepage's main job is making that depth undeniable and impossible to ignore, then getting out of the way.
4. **Restraint reads as authority.** When in doubt, cut copy and let a real quote, a real photo, or a real number carry the point.
5. **No retirement framing.** Every section, including historical ones, should imply continuation, not closure.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG 2.1 AA target: sufficient color contrast against both the cream and ink/oxblood backgrounds, visible focus states, alt text on all production photography, no motion that can't be reduced via `prefers-reduced-motion`. No additional accessibility requirements specified.
